Section 1

What are computers?

Computers are electronic machines that receive data, process instructions, and produce results. They help people solve problems, store information, communicate, design products, and automate tasks.

In the IT world, computers are essential because they run software, connect networks, manage databases, and support nearly every digital service we use every day.

Section 2

How do they work?

01

Input

Data enters the system through a keyboard, mouse, microphone, camera, or network.

02

Processing

The CPU follows instructions from software and performs calculations and logical operations.

03

Storage

RAM keeps active data ready for quick access, while SSDs or hard drives save files for later use.

04

Output

The result appears on a screen, printer, speaker, or another connected device.

Section 3

What is the most important part?

Many people call the CPU the most important part of a computer because it acts like the brain of the system. It executes instructions, coordinates tasks, and helps all the other components work together.

However, a computer is strongest when its parts work as one system: the CPU, RAM, storage, motherboard, power supply, cooling, and software all depend on each other.

Section 4

What are the components?

CPU

Processes instructions and manages operations.

Motherboard

Connects all hardware parts and allows communication.

RAM

Stores active data temporarily for fast performance.

Storage

Saves the operating system, programs, and user files.

GPU

Handles graphics, video, and visual processing.

Power Supply

Delivers electricity to every internal component.

Cooling

Controls temperature and protects hardware from overheating.

Input / Output

Lets users send commands and receive results.
